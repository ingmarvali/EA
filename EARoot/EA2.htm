<b> The Trade Register </b> <br/> In the Trade Register, companies and legal persons are registered that participate in economic traffic in the Netherlands. This way you have legal certainty when doing business. In the Trade Register you see with whom you are dealing with, who can sign on behalf of the company and whether there might be a bankruptcy. This gives you certainty when doing business. The Chamber of Commerce Act stipulates that keeping the legal trade register is one of the main tasks of the Chamber of Commerce. <br/> <br/> For more information: <a href = "file: // file: /// \ win.k94.kvk.nl \ Department $ $ projects \ customer retention \ 04.%20Domein%20 Designs \ Catalogi \ 01%20 DataCata LOGUS \ New%20 Texts \ http: \ https: \ www.kvk.nl \ over-te-kvk \ over-the-trading register \ of-power-trading register \ "> <font Color = "#0000FFF"> <u> https://www.kvk.nl/over-de-kvk/over-Herelsregister/van-kracht-handelsregisterwet/ </font> </a> <br/> <br/> <b> <br/> </b> the trade register laws law is arranged Trade Register Act. <b> </b> With the Trade Register Act 2007, Dutch law refers to the law of 22 March 2007, containing rules regarding a basic register of companies and legal persons, which entered into force on 1 July 2008. <br/> <br/> According to the Trade Register Act, the Chamber of Commerce: <BR/>>
<li> Entrepreneurs and private individuals can find out with the Trade Register with whom they do business, for example (potential) customers and suppliers. This promotes legal certainty in trade. </li> <li> Provide general information about the composition of companies and legal persons. </li> <li> Governments can remove information about companies and legal entities from the Trade Register, and they no longer have to request from the entrepreneur. That reduces the administrative burden. </li> </ul>
