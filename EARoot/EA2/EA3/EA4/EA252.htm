<b>explanation:</b><br/>A special legal situation is a situation in which the person can no longer meet his/her financial obligations. Another term that is often used for the special legal situation is insolvency. Four forms of insolvency can be registered in the Trade Register:<br/><br/><ol>
<li> Bankruptcy </li> <li> Suspension of payment </li> <li> Agreement procedure outside of bankruptcy </li> <li> debt restructuring </li> </l>
<br/>The first three can occur in all persons including {foreign companies}. The latter only applies to {natural persons}. A European regulation makes it possible to declare bankruptcy for, for example, a Belgian BVBA. <br/><br/>This class indicates the classification of the types of special legal situations that can be registered with a person (natural person, partnership, legal person or foreign company).<br/>
