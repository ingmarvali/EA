The Trade Register
The Trade Register Contains Registered Businesses and Legal Entities That Participate in Economic Activities in The Netherlands. This provides Legal Certainty When Doing Business. In The Trade Register, You Can See Who You Are Dealing with, Who is Authorized To Sign On Behalf Of The Company, And Whether there Might Be A Bankruptcy. This gives you certainty when Doing Business. The Chamber of Commerce Act Stipulates That Maintdinging the Statutory Trade Register is One of the Main Tasks of the Chamber of Commerce.

For More Information:Https: //www. Chamber of commerce. En/about-the-chamber of commerce/about-the-trade-register/force-trade-register-law/


The Trade Register Act
The Trade Register is Regulated by the Trade Register Act. The Trade Register Act 2007 refers to the act of March 22, 2007, in Dutch Law, Contouing Rules Regarding a Basic Register of Businesses and Legal Entities, which Came Into Effect on July 1, 2008.

Accordance to the Trade Register Act, The Chamber of Commerce Must Register This Data In The Trade Register for the Following Purposes:
  • Entrepreneurs and individuals can use the trade register to find out whoy they doe doing business with, such as (potential) customers and suppliers. This promotes legal certainty in trade.
  • Provide general information about the composition of businesses and legal entities.
  • Government agencies can retrieve data about companies and legal entities from the trade register and no longer need to request it from the entrepreneur. This reduces administrative burdens.


Data catalog
This Data Catalog of the Trade Register Describes The Content of the Registration as It Will Apply Upon its Statutory Introduction in 2010. As Such, The Catalog Reflects The Regulations Regulations. Finally, This Catalog is also Integrated Into the National Catalog of the System of Basic Registrations. Where Possible, Alignment with Existing Legal Definitions Has Been Sought. This Version Uses Legislation Valid in August 2010. Information about the 'Status Under Investigation' is not yet included in the catalog because functionality has not yet leg implemented in the trade register.

The Data Catalog of the Trade Register Describes which Data From The Trade Register is available. It describes the coherence and structure of this data in detail. The Chamber of Commerce sacrifices this data in Products, E.G., HR Data Service Registration. All Chamber of Commerce Products are build from and with the data from the Data Catalog.


System of Basic Registrations
The Trade Register is One of the 11 Basic Registrations. A Basic Registration is an official designated registration by the government containing data that all government institutions are required to use when performing public law tasks.

Database Rights
Database Rights to the Trade Register and the Data Contained Therein Are Exclusively Held by the Chamber of Commerce. The Chamber of Commerce Expresly Reserves All Database Rights. The Database Rights are also included in the Terms of Use That Apply to HR Data Service.

The Trade Register Distinguishes between Public and Non-Public Data. Various Laws Regulation who May view which data. For this Purpose, Trade Register Data Service HAS Data Authorization Levels:

Recipients with this Authorization Receive:
