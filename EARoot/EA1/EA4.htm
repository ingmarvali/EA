The Trade Register
The Trade Register contains registered businesses and legal entities that participate in economic activities in the Netherlands. This provides legal certainty when doing business. In the Trade Register, you can see who you are dealing with, who is authorized to sign on behalf of the company, and whether there might be a bankruptcy. This gives you certainty when doing business. The Chamber of Commerce Act stipulates that maintaining the statutory Trade Register is one of the main tasks of the Chamber of Commerce.

For more information: https://www.kvk.nl/over-de-kvk/over-het-handelsregister/van-kracht-handelsregisterwet/


The Trade Register Act
The Trade Register is regulated by the Trade Register Act. The Trade Register Act 2007 refers to the Act of March 22, 2007, in Dutch law, containing rules regarding a basic register of businesses and legal entities, which came into effect on July 1, 2008.

According to the Trade Register Act, the Chamber of Commerce must register this data in the Trade Register for the following purposes:
  • Entrepreneurs and individuals can use the Trade Register to find out who they are doing business with, such as (potential) customers and suppliers. This promotes legal certainty in trade.
  • Provide general information about the composition of businesses and legal entities.
  • Government agencies can retrieve data about companies and legal entities from the Trade Register and no longer need to request it from the entrepreneur. This reduces administrative burdens.


Data Catalog
This Data Catalog of the Trade Register describes the content of the registration as it will apply upon its statutory introduction in 2010. As such, the catalog reflects the registration regulations. Finally, this catalog is also integrated into the National Catalog of the system of basic registrations. Where possible, alignment with existing legal definitions has been sought. This version uses legislation valid in August 2010. Information about the 'status under investigation' is not yet included in the catalog because this functionality has not yet been implemented in the Trade Register.

The Data Catalog of the Trade Register describes which data from the Trade Register is available. It describes the coherence and structure of this data in detail. The Chamber of Commerce offers this data in products, e.g., HR Data Service Registration. All Chamber of Commerce products are built from and with the data from the Data Catalog.


System of Basic Registrations
The Trade Register is one of the 11 basic registrations. A basic registration is an officially designated registration by the government containing data that all government institutions are required to use when performing public law tasks.

For more information:
Government parties: www.kvk.nl/aansluiten
Market parties: https://www.kvk.nl/producten-bestellen/handelsregister-dataservice/


Database Rights
Database rights to the Trade Register and the data contained therein are exclusively held by the Chamber of Commerce. The Chamber of Commerce expressly reserves all database rights. The database rights are also included in the Terms of Use that apply to HR Data Service.


Public and Non-Public Data
The Trade Register distinguishes between public and non-public data. Various laws regulate who may view which data. For this purpose, Trade Register Data Service has data authorization levels:

1. Public data (authorization: market)
Recipients with this authorization receive:
  • public Trade Register data
Article 21 of the Trade Register Act states which data may be viewed by anyone.
For more information: https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0021777/2019-01-01#Hoofdstuk4

2. Public data including protected addresses (authorization: notaries, lawyers, and bailiffs)
Recipients with this authorization receive:
  • public data from the Trade Register
  • data from a protected address
